How to Use Hair Identifier Spray for Zero Irritation

Dermatologist-Approved Steps

Hair identifier spray is blowing up on TikTok (#hairidentifierspray has 100M views), but using it wrong can leave you red and blotchy. Board-certified dermatologist Dr. Mona Foad walked us through the fool-proof routine below.

Step-by-Step Guide

1

Double-cleanse

Start with micellar water first, then follow with a gentle foaming cleanser. This ensures your skin is completely free of makeup, oil, and impurities that could interfere with the spray.

2

Pat completely dry

Gently pat your skin dry with a clean towel. Any residual moisture will dilute the film-forming formula, making it less effective at highlighting fine hairs.

3

Shake and spray

Shake the can vigorously for 5 seconds, then hold it 6-8 inches away from your face. This ensures proper mixing and an even application.

4

Create a light white cast

Mist until you see a light white cast (3-4 seconds of spraying). Work in zones—cheeks, jaw, forehead—to ensure complete coverage without over-applying.

5

Dermaplane carefully

Using a fresh blade, dermaplane in short, downward strokes following the direction of hair growth. Never go over the same area repeatedly.

6

Complete your routine

Rinse off any residue, pat your skin dry, and apply a fragrance-free moisturizer to soothe and protect your newly revealed skin.

Pro Tips From a Dermatologist

  • Skip active ingredients: Avoid retinoids, AHAs, and BHAs 48 hours before and after dermaplaning to prevent irritation.
  • Replace blades regularly: Use a fresh blade every 3 uses to prevent micro-tears and bacterial transfer.
  • Patch test if needed: If you have eczema or rosacea, patch-test on your inner arm first to check for reactions.
  • Time it right: Perform dermaplaning in the evening to allow your skin to recover overnight without exposure to environmental stressors.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Spraying too close: This creates blotchy patches and uneven coverage. Always maintain 6-8 inches of distance.
  • Re-shaving the same spot: Going over the same area multiple times leads to razor burn and irritation.
  • Skipping moisturizer: This can cause rebound oiliness and compromise your skin barrier post-treatment.
  • Using dirty tools: Always sanitize your tools before use to prevent bacterial transfer and breakouts.
