Ingredient Breakdown (What Derms Say)

Corn / Rice Starch

Safe opacifiers, non-comedogenic. These natural ingredients create the visible cast that highlights fine hairs without clogging pores.

Niacinamide

Anti-inflammatory, fades post-shave hyperpigmentation. Helps reduce redness and prevents dark spots after hair removal.

Hyaluronic Acid

Boosts slip so blades glide, reduces tugging. Provides hydration and creates a smoother surface for the razor to glide across.

Oat Extract

Soothes mast-cell reactions in sensitive skin. Calms irritation and is especially beneficial for those with eczema or rosacea.

Propellants

Dermatologist says fine in rinse-off context; avoid if you have rosacea or active eczema flare. Butane/propane help create the fine mist application.

How to Use for Zero Irritation

Double-cleanse, pat dry. Start with completely clean skin to prevent bacteria from being pushed into pores during dermaplaning.

Shake can 5 sec; hold 6-8 in away. Proper shaking ensures even distribution of active ingredients, and the right distance prevents over-application.

Mist until a light white film appears (3-4 sec). You want enough product to see hairs but not so much that it creates a thick, difficult-to-work-with layer.

Dermaplane in short downward strokes. Use a clean, sharp blade and work in the direction of hair growth to minimize irritation.

Rinse residue, apply fragrance-free moisturizer. Thoroughly remove all product residue and follow with a calming, hydrating moisturizer.

Dr. Foad tip: Skip retinoids 48 h before and after dermaplaning to prevent excessive irritation and sensitivity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will hair grow back thicker?
No. Blunt edge illusion only; growth rate unchanged. When you shave hair, it creates a blunt tip that may feel coarser initially, but the actual thickness and growth rate remain the same.
Can I use it on eyebrows?
Too close to the eye—stick to forehead, cheeks, jawline. The product is not formulated for use near the eyes and could cause irritation or damage if it comes into contact with them.
Travel restrictions?
Aerosol cans >3.4 oz cannot go in carry-on; pack in checked luggage. Be sure to check TSA guidelines and airline policies before traveling with your hair identifier spray.
